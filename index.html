Introduction

In this project, I will provide a rebuttal to the Expressionist position as laid out in "Music and Language" by Steven Feld and Aaron A. Fox. The expressionist position says that "music communicates (whether syntactically or referentially) within the domain of human emotion, by contrast with language's capacity to communicate about conceptualization" (Feld & Fox). This definition narrowly confines the communicative abilities of both language and music. I will provide a series of examples that blur the line between language and music and show that music can communicate about conceptualization and language can communicate within the domain of human emotion.

The Balafon

Video will go here

The balafon is a type of xylophone that is commonly played in many West African countries. It is an important cultural tradition of the Sambla people in Burkina Faso. They use it not only for music but also as a musical surrogate language. This type of language encodes a linguistic message into a non-linguistic modality, in this case, the balafon (McPherson). Watch the video on the left for an example of balafon playing.

The balafon is a musical instrument, but the Sambla people also use it for linguistic communication. It can encode the same linguistic messages that the Sambla people's language can. Even though it is communicated through a musical instrument, it is still able to communicate about conceptualization.

The Visuospatial Modality

Video will go here

The visuospatial modality is comprised of manual gestures, facial expressions, and body posture (Stivers & Sidnell). It includes many non-verbal aspects of linguistic messages that are important in communicating the intended meaning (Stivers & Sidnell). Some aspects of the visuospatial modality communicate more directly with emotions than conceptualization.

Sarcasm, for example, is communicated through language, but it communicates more directly with human emotions than with conceptualization. Sarcasm is communicated through tone of voice as well as facial or manual gestures. The verbal modality (the words coming out of the speaker's mouth) contribute to conceptualization, while aspects of the visuospatial modality contribute to the emotional component of the communication that lets the listener know that the speaker is being sarcastic.

Memorization in Qur'anic Schools

Video will go here

In Northern Cameroon, students in Qur'anic schools recite verses from the Qur'an without knowing the meaning of the words they are saying. The focus is on memorizing the words and the sounds instead of the meanings, because they are learning Arabic in a religious context and not a conversational one (Moore).

While Arabic can definitely be used to communication about conceptualization, in this context it does not. The students themselves do not know the meaning of the words they are reciting, but they do know the context surrounding the text (when to say it, what the purpose is, etc.) and the emotional component resulting from that. When the students recite the Qur'anic verses, the Arabic that they speak does not communicate about conceptualization. It can, however, communicate more directly with human emotions because the students understand the emotional and cultural significance behind the text.
